Why DeployCraft.io exists

Most DevSecOps content is either too shallow to apply in production or too theoretical to ship. After years working across development, quality operations, and security — I built DeployCraft.io because the content I needed did not exist in one place.

Every guide is written from real-world experience: the same problems encountered hardening Kubernetes clusters, securing CI/CD pipelines, managing infrastructure at scale, and assessing systems from both the engineering and security perspective. Content spans the full spectrum — cluster hardening, RBAC, admission webhooks, and supply chain integrity through to red and blue team toolchains, SAST/DAST integration, secret management, and pipeline attack surfaces.

Content is structured as linked pairs — Part 1 builds the foundation, Part 2 takes it to production — so you always leave with something deployable, not just something readable. Whether you are building, securing, or breaking systems, the goal is the same: depth you can actually use.
